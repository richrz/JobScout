## 2. The Ageism Problem (Our Enemy)

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Workers 50+ experiencing workplace ageism | 78% | AARP |
| Fewer callbacks for older workers (identical CVs) | 50% | University of California |
| AI tools more likely to filter out 40+ candidates | 30% | MIT 2021 |
| Average job search duration for 50+ | 22 weeks | Labor statistics |
| Average job search duration for younger workers | ~12 weeks | Labor statistics |
| Indeed's label for 55-65 workers | "Decline" phase | Publicly called out |
| Women in high-exposure automation roles | 4.7% | ILO |
| Men in high-exposure automation roles | 2.4% | ILO |

### Key Insight
AI recruitment tools are learning discrimination from historical hiring data. They're digitizing old-school, youth-obsessed practices.

## 3. The Opportunity (Why Experience Wins)

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Age-diverse teams more likely to capture new markets | 70% | Harvard Business Review |
| Age-diverse teams more likely to improve market share | 45% | HBR |

| Turnover rates for experienced workers | Lower | Multiple studies |
| Loyalty metrics | Higher | Multiple studies |

### The Irony
Companies discriminating against experienced professionals are shooting themselves in the foot. The data proves age diversity is a competitive advantage.

## 4. Competitor Landscape

### AI-Specific Job Boards
| Competitor | Jobs | Traffic | Focus |
|------------|------|---------|-------|
| aijobs.ai | 20,000+ | 30K monthly | AI/ML/Data Science |
| Various niche boards | Limited | Low | Technical roles only |

### Generalist Boards
- **LinkedIn:** Volume-focused, algorithm-driven, ageist by proxy
- **Indeed:** Stale posts, keyword spam, "Decline" phase labeling
- **Glassdoor:** Company reviews, limited AI focus

### Gaps Identified
1. **NO ONE** serves experienced professionals pivoting to AI
2. **No one** addresses age discrimination directly
3. **No "confidence/leverage" positioning** - just listings
4. **No career transition guidance** for domain experts entering AI
5. **No resume optimization** that weaponizes experience

## 5. Target User Psychology

### Primary Persona: "The Experienced Outsider"
- **Age:** 40-60
- **Background:** 15-30 years domain expertise (sales, ops, product, engineering)
- **Situation:** Trying to break into AI roles, getting filtered out
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated, demoralized, questioning their relevance
- **Core fear:** "I'm too old and the algorithms know it"
- **Core desire:** Proof that their experience is an asset, not a liability

### What They Need
1. **Validation:** "You're not under-qualified. You're under-represented."
2. **Strategy:** How to position experience as AI-relevant
3. **Access:** Fresh roles before they go cold
4. **Confidence:** Auto-tailored materials that make them feel competitive

Not "another job board" but "the weapon against algorithmic ageism"
