# AI Careers Direction: Company-in-a-Box PRD (Reset)

## Why this reset
- The scope got too wide. This doc narrows to a focused wedge that is buildable, sellable, and expandable.
- The goal is a profitable, durable business, not a sprawling job board.

## One-line vision
- The most trusted place to find and grow into AI-powered careers.

## North-star outcome
- People discover AI-relevant roles faster and with more confidence than on generic job boards.

## Scope guardrails (non-negotiables)
- Only AI-relevant roles. If a job is not materially AI-related, it does not ship.
- Freshness matters more than volume. Quality beats quantity.
- Every feature must reduce time-to-application or increase confidence.

## Target users (first 12 months)
- AI-curious software and data professionals transitioning into AI.
- AI-native engineers and researchers who want better filters and recency.
- AI business roles (PM, sales, consulting) that get buried elsewhere.

## Core problems to solve
- AI roles are buried in generic boards, mixed with noise and stale posts.
- Most platforms cannot filter by AI tech stack or AI domain.
- Career transitions into AI are unclear and lack guidance.
- Job freshness is unreliable, leading to wasted effort.

## Value proposition
- AI-only curation: every listing proves AI relevance.
- Stack-level discovery: find roles by AI tools and domains.
- Clear pathways: show skill gaps and adjacent roles.
- Fresh results: users see openings before they go cold.

## Product wedge (MVP)
- AI relevance filter and taxonomy that removes noise.
- Core search and filters: role, seniority, location/remote, AI domain, stack.
- Job quality signals: recency, role clarity, AI evidence highlights.
- Simple skill-gap guidance for transitions.
- Alerts for saved searches (daily or weekly).

## Not now (explicitly out of scope)
- Full application tracking suite.
- Resume builder or interview prep.
- Employer marketplace or hiring CRM.
- Community features.

## Differentiation
- AI relevance scoring, not keyword matching.
- Cross-industry AI roles, not just engineering.
- Freshness-first surfacing with transparent recency signals.
- Career-path framing, not just listings.

## Data strategy (strategic, not technical)
- Own the database to control cost, speed, and features.
- Use enterprise data providers with redistribution rights.
- Tiered refresh: high-value AI roles update more often; long tail updates less.
- Deduplicate and normalize to avoid spam and cross-posting noise.

## Market analysis (directional)
- Market forces: AI adoption is spreading across functions, not just engineering.
- Buyer behavior: professionals pay for clarity, speed, and higher interview hit rate.
- Competitive landscape: generic boards prioritize volume, niche boards lack depth.
- Gap: no trusted AI-only destination with stack-level filters and career pathways.
- Timing: strong demand for AI transition clarity; low trust in generic listings.

## Business model
- B2C subscription with a generous free tier and paid tiers for power features.
- Long-term B2B: verified employer slots, sponsored roles, hiring insights.

## Go-to-market
- Start where AI career conversations already live: newsletters, communities, bootcamps.
- Publish weekly AI role trend reports and salary/stack insights to earn trust.
- Partnerships: AI learning platforms, certification providers, and alumni networks.

## Moat and defensibility
- Data quality and AI relevance signals that improve with usage.
- AI career pathway knowledge that competitors do not map.
- Trust brand built on freshness and clarity, not volume.

## Risks and mitigations
- Data rights risk: choose vendors with clear redistribution terms.
- Relevance errors: allow feedback loops and transparent labeling.
- Two-sided trap: avoid building employer features before job-seeker traction.
- Privacy: do not store resumes unless needed; clear data handling policy.

## 90-day focus plan
- Days 1-30: define taxonomy, relevance rules, and a narrow MVP experience.
- Days 31-60: add alerts and skill-gap guidance, refine relevance.
- Days 61-90: introduce paid tier and validate pricing.

## Success metrics
- Leading: search-to-save rate, alert click-through, repeat visits.
- Lagging: paid conversion, retention, interview rate (user-reported).

## Open assumptions
- Users will pay for clarity and recency if the AI relevance filter is trusted.
- The AI career transition story resonates beyond engineers.
- A focused AI-only board can beat generic boards on conversion.

# Company-in-a-Box Blueprint

## Product
- A focused AI careers search experience with clear relevance and trust signals.

## Data asset
- A clean, deduplicated AI-relevant job dataset with explainable tags.

## Distribution
- Community-first launch: newsletters, creators, bootcamps, and AI learning partners.

## Revenue
- B2C subscriptions first; B2B hiring products later.

## Operations
- Single-founder friendly: automate ingestion, moderation, and alerts.

## Brand
- The trusted map for AI careers, not just another job board.

# One-Question-at-a-Time Discovery Script

Use these in order, one question per reply:
1) Who is the single most valuable user for the first 90 days?
2) What single result would make that user say, "This is worth paying for"?
3) What category of AI roles do we exclude on purpose at launch?
4) What does "AI-relevant" mean in one sentence for this product?
5) What is the one feature that proves trust in the first 60 seconds?
6) What is the smallest paid feature you would charge for?
7) What would make you say the MVP failed?
